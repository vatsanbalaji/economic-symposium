How it works

Getting your research in front of readers is simpler than you think. Here's the process from start to finish.

01

Write your paper

Pick a topic you're curious about, form a research question, gather sources, and write up your findings. Any length works — from short policy briefs to full papers.

02

Submit the form

Fill out our submission form below with your paper details, a brief abstract, and your file. The whole process takes under five minutes.

03

Editorial review

Our editorial team reads every submission for originality, clarity, and intellectual merit. We respond within 3–4 weeks with a decision and feedback.

04

Get published

Accepted papers are featured in the Econoscope journal and shared with our growing readership of students, educators, and enthusiasts.

Frequently asked

Quick answers to common questions about submitting to Econoscope.

Do I need to be an expert in economics to submit?
Not at all. Econoscope is built for high school students — all experience levels are welcome. What matters is a genuine question, honest research, and clear writing. Your fresh perspective as a student is exactly what makes your work valuable.
What length should my paper be?
We accept papers from 800 words (short policy briefs or opinion pieces) all the way to 8,000+ words for long-form research. Most successful submissions fall in the 1,500–4,000 word range. Quality matters far more than length.
Does my paper need citations and a bibliography?
Yes — any factual claims or data should be cited. We accept any standard format (MLA, APA, Chicago). Papers without citations for factual claims will be asked to revise before full review.
Can I submit a paper I wrote for a class?
Yes! Papers written for school assignments are welcome, as long as you are the original author and submitting doesn't violate any agreements with your school or teacher.
Can I submit with a co-author?
Absolutely. List the primary author in the submission form and include all co-authors and their schools in the co-authors field. The paper can also list them on the title page.
What happens after I submit?
You'll receive an automatic confirmation email. The editorial team will review your paper and respond within 3–4 weeks with one of: accept, revise & resubmit, or decline with feedback.
